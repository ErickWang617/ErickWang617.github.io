Steak & Potatoes

One of the most well known and simple dishes in America is steak. In this recipe, we will learn how to make a simple but delicious meal! The required ingredients and steps are listed below. Feel free to swap out any ingredients for alternatives. Although this dish is simple, cooking steak requires the chef to pay close attention and good timing.

Needed Ingredients

  • Chuck eye center cut *
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Steak Seasoning
  • Garlic
  • Butter
  • Thyme
  • Mushrooms
  • Yukon Gold Potatoes

* Any preferred cut of steak can work for this recipe. However, cooking time and seasoning amount will vary.

Many ingredients are interchangable. For instance, we can add or swap in broccolini, asparagus, or even roasted carrots.

Steak and potatoes with mushrooms and minced garlic

Steps:

  1. First boil enough water to encapsulate potatoes.
  2. Salt the water and then gently drop potatoes into the pot and put the heat on low.
  3. Let potatoes cook for 20-25 minutes. In the meanwhile, start preparing the other ingredients.
  4. Season each side of the steak, including the sides, with salt, black pepper, and the steak seasoning of your choice.
  5. Wash vegetables, chop mushroom into slices, and mince garlic.
  6. Bring pan to medium heat and then add enough oil to cover most of the surface.
  7. Gently place steak into the pan and let it sear for 2 minutes or until a litle less than half of the width of the steak has been cooked.
  8. Add 1-2 tablespoons of butter and flip the steak over to the other side.
  9. Add thyme and minced garlic on top of the steak and begin basting the melted butter onto the steak at an angle using a high heat resistant spoon.
  10. Add in the mushrooms and finished potatoes, which you can cut into halves, into the pan
  11. After steak has a brown crust, flip the steak onto its sides using tongs and sear each side for 10-30 seconds.
  12. Check steak with meat thermometer and take it out depending on your preference (rare, medium, etc).
  13. Plate meal and enjoy it!
